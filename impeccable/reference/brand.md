# Brand register

When design IS the product: brand sites, landing pages, marketing surfaces, campaign pages, portfolios, long-form content, about pages. The deliverable is the design itself; a visitor's impression is the thing being made.

## The brand slop test

If someone could look at this and say "AI made that" without hesitation, it's failed. The bar is distinctiveness.

## Typography

### Font selection procedure

1. Read the brief. Write three concrete brand-voice words (physical-object words, not "modern" or "elegant").
2. List the three fonts you'd reach for by reflex. If any appear in the reflex-reject list below, reject them.
3. Browse a real catalog with the three words in mind. Find the font for the brand as a *physical object*.
4. Cross-check. "Elegant" is not necessarily serif. "Technical" is not necessarily sans.

### Reflex-reject list

Fraunces . Newsreader . Lora . Crimson . Crimson Pro . Crimson Text . Playfair Display . Cormorant . Cormorant Garamond . Syne . IBM Plex Mono . IBM Plex Sans . IBM Plex Serif . Space Mono . Space Grotesk . Inter . DM Sans . DM Serif Display . DM Serif Text . Outfit . Plus Jakarta Sans . Instrument Sans . Instrument Serif

### Reflex-reject aesthetic lanes

- **Editorial-typographic.** Display serif (often italic) + small mono labels + ruled separators + monochromatic restraint. Every Stripe-adjacent and Notion-adjacent brand has landed here by 2026.

### Pairing and voice

Two families minimum is the rule *only* when the voice needs it. A single well-chosen family with committed weight/size contrast is stronger than a timid display+body pair.

### Scale

Modular scale, fluid `clamp()` for headings, ratio >=1.25 between steps.
Light text on dark backgrounds: add 0.05-0.1 to line-height.

## Color

Brand surfaces have permission for Committed, Full palette, and Drenched strategies.
- Name a real reference before picking a strategy.
- Palette IS voice.
- When the strategy is Committed or Drenched, commit. Don't hedge with neutrals.
- Don't converge across projects.

## Layout

- Asymmetric compositions are one option.
- Vary spacing for rhythm.
- For image-led briefs, full-bleed hero imagery with overlaid menu is canonical.

## Imagery

**When the brief implies imagery, you must ship imagery.** Zero images is a bug, not a design choice.

- For greenfield work without local assets, use stock imagery (Unsplash). Verify URLs before referencing.
- Search for the brand's physical object, not the generic category.
- One decisive photo beats five mediocre ones.
- Alt text is part of the voice.

## Motion

One well-orchestrated page-load beats scattered micro-interactions.

## Brand bans

- Monospace as lazy shorthand for "technical / developer."
- Large rounded-corner icons above every heading. Screams template.
- Single-family pages that picked the family by reflex, not voice.
- All-caps body copy.
- Timid palettes and average layouts. Safe = invisible.
- Zero imagery on a brief that implies imagery.
- Defaulting to editorial-magazine aesthetics on briefs that aren't magazine-shaped.
- Repeated tiny uppercase tracked labels above every section heading.

## Brand permissions

- Ambitious first-load motion.
- Single-purpose viewports. One dominant idea per fold.
- Unexpected color strategies.
- Art direction per section.
